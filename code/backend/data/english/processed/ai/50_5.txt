In conclusion, Dostoevsky's "Crime and Punishment" offers a profound meditation on the nature of human goodness and evil, posing fundamental questions about the human condition and the struggle for moral integrity in a world plagued by suffering and injustice. While the novel acknowledges the capacity for darkness within the human soul, it also affirms the potential for redemption and moral growth through acts of compassion, humility, and self-awareness. Ultimately, Dostoevsky leaves the question of mankind's inherent nature open-ended, inviting readers to grapple with the complexities of morality and conscience in their own lives.
