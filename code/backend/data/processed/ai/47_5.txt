Furthermore, Lee suggests that moral courage is a potent antidote to the "hell" of prejudice and hatred that plagues Maycomb. By challenging the status quo and standing up for what is right, characters like Atticus and Boo disrupt the cycle of bigotry and ignorance that perpetuates injustice in their community. Through their courageous actions, they offer a glimmer of hope amidst the darkness, inspiring others to confront their own biases and prejudices.
