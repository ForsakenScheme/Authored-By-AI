The damnation of the canyon is one man's account of the effects of turning a free flowing river into a damn. He uses his first hand experience as a seasonal park ranger to evaluate the transformation of the region, what he calls as its "damnation". The authorhas "had the unique opportunity to observe firsthand some of the differences between the environment of a free river and a power plant reservoir". However he has a certain bias, he admits to this, he is what most would call a "tree hugger". He identifies himself as a "butterfly chaser, a googly eyed bleeding heart and wild conservative." Making his views to his perspective of what he wants to see.
